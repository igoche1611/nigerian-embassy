Visa Services

Here you will find detailed information regarding the visa application process for travel to Nigeria. Please read all sections carefully before submitting your application. Note that visa processing times may vary, and applicants are advised to apply well in advance of their travel dates.

Types of Visas

The Embassy issues several types of visas, including Tourist/Visitor's Visas, Business Visas, Temporary Work Permits (TWP), and Subject to Regularization (STR) Visas for expatriates taking up employment in Nigeria. Each visa type has specific requirements based on the purpose of travel.

General Requirements

  • Valid passport with at least 6 months validity and two blank visa pages.
  • Completed online visa application form from the Nigeria Immigration Service portal.
  • Proof of online payment of visa fees.
  • Two recent passport-sized photographs (35mm x 45mm) on a white background.
  • Confirmed round-trip flight itinerary.
  • Letter of invitation from a host in Nigeria or a confirmed hotel reservation.

Application Process

The application process is as follows: 1. Complete the application form and pay the fee online via the Nigeria Immigration Service website. 2. Print the completed form, payment receipt, and acknowledgement slip. 3. Schedule an appointment for document submission and biometrics at the Embassy.

Passport Services

This section provides information for Nigerian citizens who wish to apply for a new passport, renew an existing one, or replace a lost/damaged passport. All passport services require a prior online application and appointment.

New Passport Application & Renewal

Applications for new passports (especially for newborns) and renewals are processed through the Nigeria Immigration Service portal. Applicants must provide proof of Nigerian citizenship, such as a birth certificate or old passport, along with a National Identification Number (NIN). An appointment for biometric capturing is mandatory.

Lost or Damaged Passport

For a lost or stolen passport, applicants must first obtain a police report from the local authorities. This report, along with an affidavit of loss, is required for the replacement application. The process is otherwise similar to a standard renewal.

Emergency Travel Certificate (ETC)

An Emergency Travel Certificate (ETC) is a one-way travel document issued to Nigerian citizens who have lost their passport and need to travel urgently to Nigeria. It is not valid for travel to any other country.

Requirements

  • Completed ETC application form.
  • A police report confirming the loss of the passport.
  • Proof of Nigerian citizenship (e.g., copy of lost passport, birth certificate).
  • Two recent passport-sized photographs.
  • Confirmed flight ticket to Nigeria.

Validity

The ETC is valid for a single journey to Nigeria and must be surrendered to immigration officials upon arrival.

Declaration of Age

The Embassy provides Declaration of Age services for Nigerian citizens who require an official document to attest to their age, often in cases where the original birth certificate is unavailable.

Procedure

Applicants must submit a sworn affidavit of age declaration from a Nigerian court, along with their Nigerian passport, passport photographs, and any other available supporting documents. An appointment is required to process the application at the Embassy.

About the Embassy

Our Mission

The Embassy of the Federal Republic of Nigeria in Prague is dedicated to strengthening the bilateral relationship between Nigeria and the Czech Republic. Our mission is to promote mutual interests in trade, investment, and cultural exchange, while diligently serving the consular needs of Nigerian citizens in the Czech Republic and providing efficient visa services to foreign nationals.

History

Diplomatic relations between Nigeria and the former Czechoslovakia were established shortly after Nigeria's independence in 1960. The Embassy in Prague has since been a vital link, fostering cooperation and friendship that has continued to grow and evolve with the Czech Republic since 1993.

About Nigeria

The Giant of Africa

Nigeria, a federal republic in West Africa, is the continent's most populous country. Its dynamic economy is one of the largest in Africa, driven by a vibrant services sector, rich natural resources, and a burgeoning tech industry. Nigeria's landscape is as diverse as its people, ranging from tropical rainforests in the south to the Sahel savannah in the north.

A Hub of Culture & Creativity

Home to over 250 ethnic groups, Nigeria is a tapestry of unique cultures, languages, and traditions. It is the heart of Nollywood, the world's second-largest film industry, and the driving force behind the global success of Afrobeats music. From the spicy flavors of Jollof rice to its world-renowned literature and art, Nigeria's cultural influence is felt worldwide.
