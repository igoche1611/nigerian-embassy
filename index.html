A Message from the CDA

On behalf of the President of the Federal Republic of Nigeria, His Excellency Bola Ahmed Tinubu, and the entire staff of the Embassy, I extend a warm welcome to the official website of the Embassy of Nigeria in Prague.

This website is designed to be a comprehensive resource for Nigerian citizens, our Czech friends, and international visitors. Whether you are seeking consular services, exploring business opportunities in Nigeria, or learning more about our rich culture, we are here to assist you.

We are committed to fostering the excellent bilateral relations between Nigeria and the Czech Republic and to providing efficient and courteous service to all. Thank you for visiting.

Consular & Visa Services

We provide a wide range of consular services. Please review the requirements for each service carefully before your visit.

Visa Services

We process applications for various categories of visas to Nigeria. All applications must be submitted through the Nigeria Immigration Service portal. Please note that the Embassy is not responsible for payments or processing on the portal.

Emergency Travel Certificate

An Emergency Travel Certificate (ETC) is issued for urgent, one-way travel to Nigeria for citizens who have lost their passport or do not have a valid one. Applicants must provide proof of Nigerian citizenship, such as a birth certificate or a previous passport copy.

Issuance of permits for the transportation of human remains to Nigeria for burial.

History & People

Nigeria, the "Giant of Africa," is a country of over 200 million people with more than 250 ethnic groups. Its history is rich with powerful kingdoms and empires. It gained independence from the United Kingdom in 1960 and has since become a federal republic, playing a significant role in African and global affairs.

Culture, Arts & Tourism

Nigerian culture is expressed through its music, dance, literature, and film. The Nigerian film industry, known as "Nollywood," is the second-largest in the world. From the sandy beaches of Lagos to the ancient city of Kano and the plateaus of Jos, Nigeria offers a wealth of tourist attractions.
