National Symbols

The National Flag

The Nigerian flag was designed in 1959 and first officially hoisted on October 1, 1960. The flag has three vertical bands of green, white, and green. The two green stripes represent Nigeria's natural wealth and rich agricultural land, while the white band in the middle represents peace and unity.

The Coat of Arms

The Nigerian Coat of Arms features a black shield with a wavy white pall, symbolizing the confluence of the Niger and Benue rivers. The black shield represents Nigeria's fertile soil. The two white horses supporting the shield represent dignity, and the eagle perched on top represents strength. The ground on which the shield stands is covered in coctus spectabilis, a common flower found throughout Nigeria. The national motto, "Unity and Faith, Peace and Progress," is displayed on a banner below.
